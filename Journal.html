Week 18:

This week, I started our journey to learning about Artificial Intelligence (AI) in our lives and technology through CodeHs. I was excited to learn more about this fascinating topic with my group, consisting of Rory and Sophia. We began by getting in groups and conducting detective work to find an AI suspect. After some research, we discovered how AI works and its impact on our daily lives. We completed two units within a two-week period, covering big topics such as the programming of AI, its effects on society, and the differences between conscious and logical AI. I learned that AI can be programmed to be either conscious or logical, and that the lack of consciousness in robots can have significant consequences. For example, robots without consciousness may not be able to make decisions based on moral principles or empathy. We also explored the concept of emotional/conscious AI, such as ChatGPT, which can mimic human behaviors. The Turing Test is another important concept we discussed, which evaluates whether a machine can exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human. In addition to these topics, we learned about Figure 01, which measures the intelligence of AI robots, and the robot Andrew, which has shown signs of consciousness. This was a fascinating topic that raised many questions about the potential consequences of creating conscious machines. Reflection Throughout this journey, I have gained a deeper understanding of AI and its impact on our lives. I was surprised by how much I didn't know about AI before starting this course. The detective work we did at the beginning was challenging but engaging, and I enjoyed working with my group to solve the mystery. I found the topics covered in this course to be both fascinating and thought-provoking. I never realized how much AI is already integrated into our daily lives and how it can be used for good or bad. The concept of conscious vs. logical AI has made me think about the ethics of creating intelligent machines. Challenges and Curiosities One challenge I faced was understanding the complex concepts of AI programming and consciousness. At times, it was difficult to wrap my head around the ideas presented in class. However, my group members were helpful in explaining things in different ways, which made it easier for me to grasp the material. A curiosity I have is what the future holds for AI development. Will we see more conscious machines? How will this impact society? These questions are just a few examples of the many concerns and opportunities that come with advancements in AI. Overall, this course has been an exciting journey into the world of Artificial Intelligence. I look forward to learning more about this topic and exploring its applications in various fields.

Multimodal AI: I explored the intersection of language and visual data, where AI models can learn to recognize patterns and make predictions based on both text and images. Using AI to create a website: We learned about Gemini, a platform that uses AI to generate website content. This was a fascinating example of how AI can be used in practical applications. In addition to these topics, I also learned about the importance of diversity and ethics in the tech industry. Some tech companies prioritize profit over these values, while others make intentional efforts to create inclusive work environments. This includes diversity training, clear anti-discrimination policies, and actively seeking diverse candidates for hiring and promotion. The case of Timnit Gebru, a researcher who was unfairly terminated from her job at Google, highlights the challenges that advocates for ethical AI face. Her story underscores the need for companies to prioritize ethics in AI development and underscores the biases that exist in AI systems.
