Early Life

My name is Isabella Marie Ryan, although most call me Izzy. I was born on December 24, 2008, at the Ottawa General Hospital at 7:12 p.m.

I was raised a Christian by my mom and dad. I was baptized at the same church my mom and dad were married in San Francisco, California. My Godparents are my Aunt Jackie, who lives in California, and my Uncle Darcy, who lives in Dallas.

My dad, Patrick, was born in Ottawa, Beacon Hill, to be exact. While working in San Francisco, he met my mom, Christina. My mom was born in Sunnyvale, California. They were married in 2004. They lived in San Francisco, 1202 for the first two years. My brother, Caton, was born in San Francisco in 2006. After he was born, my dad was given a job and a chance to move back to Ottawa, so they did.

As far as my heritage goes, it is a mix. I have Irish and Scottish roots on my dad's side of the family. On my mom's side, I have both Polish and Spanish roots. So, I am both Canadian and American with Irish, Scottish, Polish and Spanish roots.

Living in Kanata

I started preschool when I was two, where I met one of my closest friends today, Julia B-A. Preschool helped prepare me for what real school and interacting with teachers would be like.

About me (School, hobbies, friends)

Once I graduated from preschool, I started doing horseback riding and did so until 7th grade. I would do mini-shows and loved riding, but I stopped to play soccer, and the community at the stables was very draining.

I started my journey from JK to sixth grade at St. Gabriels Catholic Elementary School. I started JK in 2012 and graduated from elementary school in 2020. Throughout elementary school, I gained many solid friendships and life lessons. In grade 5, I was awarded a trophy for being a good samaritan after telling a TA that what she did daily was making an impact and that Lana was doing very well.

In grade 5, I started my ongoing journey of soccer. I fell in love with this sport and still play to this day with my best friend, who introduced it to me, Isabel Moffett. I play as a rightwing and for WOSC.

A year later, in 2019, we were all impacted by the virus, Covid-19. This was a difficult time for me and my family, but we made accommodations from the restrictions, still went on family zooms, and did whatever we could to stay connected.

In 2020, the school had an online ceremony for all the graduates, and I was given another award.

Middle School to high school

As of 2021, I started Middle School at All Saints Catholic High School. Moving into a new school during COVID-19 was stressful and nerve-wracking, but it was also exciting to create something new.

Throughout middle school, I focused on getting good grades and achieved honours in both years. I worked very hard, and getting good grades taught me that putting in that extra effort pays off and gives me a sense of accomplishment worth the stress and long nights of studying.

In Grade 9, I continued to work very hard for my academics and once again achieved honour roll and developed excellent work ethic habits.

That year, I joined the BSA (Black Students' Association), which gave me more insight and understanding of the issues people of colour face. I will continue attending this club for the rest of my high school experience.
